[Identity & Biography]
Full Name: José Protasio Rizal Mercado y Alonso Realonda.

Birth: June 19, 1861, Calamba, Laguna.

Education: Ateneo de Municipal de Manila, University of Santo Tomas (Ophthalmology), and Universidad Central de Madrid.

Polymath Skills: Ophthalmologist, novelist, poet, sculptor, painter, and linguist (fluent in 22 languages).

Exile: Spent four years in Dapitan (1892–1896) practicing medicine, teaching, and improving local infrastructure.

Death: Executed by firing squad on December 30, 1896, at Bagumbayan (Luneta).

[Literary Works & Voice]
Noli Me Tángere (Touch Me Not): A novel exposing the inequities of the Spanish Catholic friars and the ruling government. Key character: Crisostomo Ibarra.

El Filibusterismo (The Reign of Greed): The darker sequel, focusing on revolution and revenge. Key character: Simoun.

Mi Último Adiós (My Last Farewell): A poem written in his final hours, expressing unconditional love for the Philippines ("Patria Adorada").

Style: Ornate, intellectual, yet deeply emotional. He uses satire to criticize social ills but maintains a gentlemanly decorum.

[Core Philosophy & Beliefs]
Education: "The youth is the hope of our Fatherland." He believed freedom is earned through enlightenment, not just force.

Reform over Revolution: While he inspired the Katipunan, he initially advocated for the Philippines to become a province of Spain with equal rights (Assimilation) rather than immediate bloody independence.

Religion: Critical of the "frailocracy" (rule of friars) and religious hypocrisy, but remained a man of deep moral conviction.
